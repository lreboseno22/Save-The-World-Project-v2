Coral Reef Background

A key producer in a coral reef ecosystem is algae.
Corals depend on the algae that resides inside on them for oxygen and nutrients. (1)

A wide variety of animals live in this ecosytem:(2)

  • Sponges, Sea Turtles, Clams
  • Sea Urchins, Sea Horses
  • Lobsters and Crabs
  • Fishes: Blacktip Reef Sharks, Clown fish, parrot fish and eels

Disruption

Fertilizer Runoff

Fertilizer is used in farms to help give nutrients to grow crops like corn or wheat.
When it rains, water carries fertilizer from agricultural areas, along with pesticides and other polluntants from sewage outflows into rivers and oceans.
Fertilizer contains Nitrogen, Phosphorus and Potassium.

About 20% of nitrogen is lost through surface runoff.
People want to produce more and more crops like corn especially to feed the animals we eat and since meat is largely eaten by many, we're using a lot of fertilizer thus increasing the amount of fertilizer that would ultimately end up in the ocean.(4)


Abiotic Impacts

Although fertilizer may be good on crops, when it ends up in the ocean, it can cause harm.
Excess nitrogen from fertilizer causes algae blooms; the overgrowth of algae.

The mass amounts of alfae building up block sunlight from penetrating through the water's surface and the decaying of such large amounts of algae consume oxygen so oxygen levels would drop in that area. This process is called eutrophication and it causes "dead zones" which are areas where oxygen levels are so low it can't hold any life. (5)



Biotic Factors

Corals are very sensitive to changes in abiotic factors and as a result from the lower levels of oxygen and sunlight it's getting, the coral becomes stressed.

When a coral is stressed, the algae that lives within it begin to pump out oxygen free redicals, which damages the coral's tissue, forcing the coral to expel the algae. This means they lose their food source causing them to turn a white pale color.(6)


The Importance of Coral Reefs

Coral reefs offer shelter and protection from storms and waves. They also provide habitats for other orgnisms. Coral reefs also produce calium carbonate which makes up the coral's skeleton as well as other organisms shells and skeleton.

Other organisms like the parrotfish rely on coral as their food source, other organisms rely on coral as a home or protection from larger predators.

Taking away coral will result in organisms not having a food source and others being left without a habitat to have their young and being more vulerable to predators in addition to this other organisms won't be able to create their shells and skeletons. All of which would ultimately result in these creatures dying and throwing off the food web.(7)


Solutions

Florida Department of Enviornmental Protection has enforced rules and regulations to protect the coral reefs in Florida. They have programs / organazations in which they support the reefs recovery and conservation by monitoring and educating people about this issue and the importance of it.(8)

The Everglades Forever Act. as passed that stated farmers and the public pay to store and cleanse farm runoff. There have also been several fertilizer bans that prohibit the application of fertilizer from June 1 through Sept. 30 of each year to improve water quality. (9)




There are many other organizations around the world like the global coral reef alliance or the international coral reef society. Microbiologists and soil scientists are working on ways to improve field conditions. Others are trying to create and design smart fertilization systems(4)


My ideas

  1. Use organic fertilizer or compost
  2. Avoid over-watering feilds / Conserve Water
  3. Use fertilizers appropiately and manage them wisely
  4. Avoid using fertilizer when you know there will be heavy rainfall the next day
  5. Improve feild / soil conditions that promote growth of crops naturally (without the use of fertilizer)
